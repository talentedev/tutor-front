By using our Service, you consent to our collection and use of your information as described in this Privacy Policy.

  1. What information do we collect?

    We collect two basic types of information

    1. Personally identifiable information (PII): PII is information that identifies you as an individual, such as your name, email address, username, and password. You voluntarily provide this information when you establish an account. The types of PII we request depends upon whether you are registering to offer tutoring services (“Tutor”) or to obtain tutoring services (“Student”).

      1. For Students: To establish your account we only ask you to provide your name (first and last), and email address. You will then create a username and password, which we will store. You will also need to provide credit card information if you’re interested in retaining a Tutor. Once your account is established, you will be prompted to add certain personal details, such as the name(s) of children who will be students under your account.
      2. For Tutors: To become a Tutor, you will need to provide your name (first and last), date of birth, email address, phone number, resume, and documents to support your qualifications (transcripts, certificates, diplomas, etc.). You will also need to provide your social security number and bank account details in order to receive payments.

      Also, if you choose to login to our site through a social networking site, you are granting us permission to access and use certain information about you as permitted by the or social network. For example, if you use Facebook to login, it may provide us with access to whatever information you choose to include in your public profile, such as friends’ names, pictures, genders, user Ids, connections, and any other shared content.

      Finally, when you log on to your account, we will record your IP address and/or unique mobile device identifier and may tie it to your specific account. Under ordinary circumstances, an IP address recorded in isolation is not PII under the standard definition of the term. However, it may become PII when it is combined with other information, as in this case.

    2. General Information: General Information consists of information that is anonymous in nature and does not identify you as an individual. We use cookies to collect General Information. A “cookie” is a type of software used to gather and store visitor information. The General Information we collect includes your computer IP address (when not tied to your particular account), unique mobile device identifier, browser type, ISP or carrier name, and the URL of the last webpage you visited before visiting our website.

  2. How do we use the information we collect?

    1. How We Use PII: If you are a student, we use the PII we collect from and about you to enable us to provide you with our Service. We use your email address to contact you to confirm bookings, respond to customer service requests, or for other matters regarding your account. We may also send you emails to inform you about promotions and for marketing purposes. You may opt-out of receiving non-account related emails by clicking on the “unsubscribe” link located at the bottom of the email. If you are a tutor, we use your PII to process your application, verify your identity and qualifications, and to send payments to you.
    2. How We Use General Information: We use the General Information we collect to obtain insights on how people use our site and our Service. We use this information for various internal business purposes, and to ensure that our Service continues to appeal to our users.

    1. We will share your contact information with any Tutor you wish to retain. Likewise, if you are a Tutor, we may share your contact information with Students.
    2.     3. If requested or required by law enforcement authorities, courts, or regulators, we may disclose any information we have about our users. We also may disclose your PII to exercise or protect legal rights or defend against legal claims.
    4. In some cases, we may choose to buy or sell assets. In these business transfers, customer information, including PII, is typically one of the business assets that are transferred. If all or substantially all of our business assets were acquired, or in the unlikely event that we go out of business or enter into bankruptcy, customer information (including your PII), would be one of the assets that is transferred or acquired by a third party. You acknowledge that such transfers may occur, and that any assets may continue to be used according to your PII as set forth in this policy.
